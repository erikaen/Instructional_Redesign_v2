The entity stands on its own
What You Worked Out
  • The LLC has its own EIN — an identity to the outside world, separate from your SSN.
  • It has its own bank account and its own card; the business no longer rides your personal accounts.
  • Co-mingling is over — the by-hand sorting you’ve done since 1.1 is now institutional.
  • The only move that touched the books was your $1,300: a real capital contribution (Cash 0 → 1,300; members’ capital 990 → 2,290).
The deeper point

The entity is now a counterparty in its own right — its own number, its own accounts, able to owe and be owed in its own name. The boundary you first drew by hand in 1.1 is now backed by banks and the IRS. None of that was a legal-form trick: the only thing that moved the economics was your own decision to put in $1,300.
