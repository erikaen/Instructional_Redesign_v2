What You Discovered
  • The LLC has its own EIN: an identifying number the bank and IRS can use.
  • The LLC now has its own bank account and its own card.
  • The credit-card balance is now split correctly: $1,230 of business charges belongs on the LLC card, and $230 of personal charges stays on your personal card.
  • The only new amount in this sequence was the $500 cash contribution. Cash increased by $500, and the equity side increased by $500.
The Deeper Point

The LLC now has practical separation in the outside world: its own identifying number, its own bank account, and its own card. That does not mean every setup step changed the balance sheet. Getting the EIN and moving the existing $1,230 business credit-card balance into the LLC’s name changed the setup, not the recorded amount. The new economic event was the $500 cash contribution.
