A few days after Maria, the insurance agent, came by, your friend Anna stops in. Anna kept the books at City Cycle for years, and she still does her own brake jobs.

Since Maria’s visit, you took the Insurable Property list you prepared for her and entered it into your Excel workbook as a Things list. Each item has a dollar value and is grouped by category. You can collapse a category to one line or expand it to see the items inside. This is what you pull up to show Anna.

ANNA
“Nice! You actually put dollar values on everything. That’s more than most people do. But this list is still only part of the picture. Where’s the cash? We need to look at the money moving through your bank account.”
What You’ll Discover
  • The bank statement mixes the repair work with personal activity, so you have to separate them first.
  • Cash moving in and out is not the same as what the repair work has and owes.
  • Some payments create promises you still owe. Some payments create things you still have.
