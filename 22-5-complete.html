Module 2 · Getting Formal · ~10 min · Phase 5 of 5

A legal body — born today, wrapping work that’s older
What you discovered
  • A legal entity comes into existence through a filing. Once the State accepts the Certificate of Organization, a new “legal person” exists.
  • The filing changed legal status, but it did not change the operation itself: same tools, same customers, same work today.
  • The filing, by itself, did not change the balance sheet. The recorded assets, recorded liabilities, and the difference between them are still the same.
  • The legal body is new. The accounting boundary you drew in Module 1 came first.
  • The LLC carries your name, but it is now legally separate from you.
The deeper point

The Certificate gave the repair work a legal body, but it did not create the repair work.

You were already operating, drawing a boundary, and keeping books for the repair work before the LLC existed. Now there are two different kinds of separation: the accounting boundary in the records, and the legal body created by the filing. They are related, but they are not the same thing.
