What You Worked Out
  • You wrote your own Operating Agreement — City Cycle’s (the one Anna set up) was only an example.
  • It stays a draft for now — Schedule A attests to what’s contributed, and the contributing isn’t finished. You complete the schedule, then sign, at the end of the module.
  • On June 1, the Company received $4,710 of resources and rights and took on $2,250 of claims. The $2,460 difference became Member’s Capital, all Contributed; Generated began at $0.
  • Limited liability put a wall between the Company’s debts and your personal life — who can be pursued, not what the records show.
  • And the keystone: Module 1’s information boundary lived in your personal records. The legal body and the Company’s books are new on June 1.
The deeper point

Accounting boundaries and legal boundaries answer different questions. Before June 1, your personal records tracked the repair work separately. On June 1, the LLC and its own books began; the Company begins its story with the formation rows, not the May work rows.

Coming Next — 2.4: The Entity’s Own Number, and Stopping the Co-mingling
