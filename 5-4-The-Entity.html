Go back to the very first module. Before any of this, you did one quiet thing: you drew a line.

This stand, this card, this part of the bank account — part of the repair work. Your groceries, your gas, your paycheck — not part of the repair work.

For every item, you were really asking the same question: is this part of the repair work, or is this personal?

ANNA
“That line has a name. The thing your records are about — the thing the Assets and Liabilities belong to — is the entity.

And here is the part people often miss: you did not find the entity. You made it by deciding what is inside the line and what is outside it. The entity is a point of view you use for a purpose.”
The boundary is the work; the name follows

An entity is anything you choose to keep records for: this repair work, a person, a school, a food bank.

It is not the whole bicycle industry, because that would include too many different entities at once. It is not Smith’s deposit, because a deposit is something that happens to an entity. It is not “last May,” because that is a period of time, not the thing the records are about.

If you draw the line in the wrong place, every number after that answers the wrong question.
