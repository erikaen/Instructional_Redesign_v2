Module 5: Naming What You Built

Bicycle repair shop · ~15 min

Go back to the very first session. Before any of this, you did one quiet thing: you drew a line. This stand, this card, this slice of the bank account — part of the repair work. Your groceries, your gas, your paycheck — not. You kept asking the same question of every item: is this the repair work, or is this me?

ANNA
“That line you drew has a name. The subject of a set of books — the thing the Assets and Liabilities belong to — is the entity. And here’s the part people miss: you didn’t find it. You made it, by deciding what’s in and what’s out. The entity is a perspective you adopt for a purpose, not an object sitting in the world.”
The boundary is the work; the name follows

An entity is anything you can draw a clear edge around for a reason — this repair work, a person, a school, a food bank. It isn’t the bicycle industry (too many entities at once), or Smith’s deposit (that’s something that happens to an entity), or “last May” (a slice of time). Draw the line in the wrong place and every number after it is answering the wrong question.
