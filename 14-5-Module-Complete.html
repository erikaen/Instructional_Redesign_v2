What You Discovered
  • You kept a Reasons tab — one row for the why behind each change to a list.
  • Tallying the Cash list forced a discovery: the flows came out −$160, yet your bank account never went negative — your personal self had quietly covered the difference. The $160 got its row, and its reason, only once you saw it.
  • Most reasons came in pairs — same event, two changes. You didn’t arrange that; it showed up on its own.
  • The matched pairs just shuffle — Assets and Liabilities move together, so the difference doesn’t budge.
  • The reasons that move the difference are two plain kinds: what you contributed and what the work generated.
