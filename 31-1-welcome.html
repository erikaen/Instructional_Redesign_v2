Module 3 · The Story Between the Snapshots · ~10 min · Phase 1 of 5

A Season Goes By

In Module 2 you made the repair work into a legal body: Bike Repair, LLC. The day it became formal, your books — Bike-Repair.xlsx — showed a single balance sheet, one snapshot of the new entity:

Then summer came. You kept your day job and worked the shop in the evenings and on weekends — part-time, but busy. Repairs brought money in. Parts and rent got used up. You bought a new tool and took some cash out for yourself. Beside each change, you jotted a short Reason, the way you learned to back in Module 1.

Now it’s the end of August, and Anna — who set up your books when you formed the LLC — checks back in. “I set up your books,” she says. “Now let’s read them. A season has gone by — let’s see how the shop did.”

What you’ll discover
  • How the shop’s numbers looked after a full summer of repairs — and how that compares to the day you opened.
  • Why a single fresh snapshot, on its own, leaves the most important question unanswered.
  • What a whole season’s worth of Reasons is quietly pushing you toward.
