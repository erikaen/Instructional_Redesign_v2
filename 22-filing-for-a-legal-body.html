Tutorial 2: Filing for a Legal Body

Module 2 · Getting Formal · ~10 min

You left Maria wanting one thing: a legal wall between the repair work and your own pocket — the kind a policy can’t give you. So you call Anna — she kept the books at City Cycle for years and helped the owner set it up as an LLC.

She doesn’t overthink it.

ANNA
“Form an LLC. That’s the wall. I set one up with the owner at City Cycle — we used an attorney back then; these days you could just have an AI assistant walk you through it and check it against the state — honestly, it’s a couple of forms.”

“Start with the one that actually brings it into existence. After that, it’s a legal person — and the claims that scared you stop at its door, not yours.”
What You’ll Work Out
  • What it actually takes to bring a legal entity into existence.
  • What changes the instant it exists — and what, surprisingly, doesn’t.
  • Which is older: the legal body, or the operation it wraps.

First, your LLC needs a name. Until now it’s just been “the repair work” or “the operation.” Now it gets one of its own — and it’ll carry your name, even as it becomes legally separate from you.

A legal person was just born. That feels like a big change. So walk the shop and check: what actually moved? Tap each one.

A legal body — born today, wrapping work that’s older
What You Worked Out
  • A legal entity is born by a filing — one page, and a new legal person exists.
  • The instant it existed, nothing about the operation changed: same tools, same customers, same balance sheet.
  • The legal line is brand-new; the accounting line you drew back in 1.1 is far older.
  • Your shop carries your name — yet it’s now legally a separate person from you.
The deeper point

The Certificate gave the operation a legal body — but it didn’t create the operation. You’ve kept these books, this boundary, since 1.1. There’s a legal line and an accounting line, and they’re not the same line. Next, they finally get a name.
