Tutorial 4: The Entity’s Own Number

Module 2 · Getting Formal · ~10 min · Phase 1 of 5

An Identity of Its Own

The LLC exists, it has rules, and the existing repair business has been contributed to it. But to open accounts in the LLC’s name, the outside world needs one more thing: the LLC’s own identifying number. You go to open a business bank account, and the bank stops you:

THE BANK
“We can’t open this under your Social Security number. We need the LLC’s number — its EIN (Employer Identification Number).”
Until now, the repair work has run through your personal checking account and personal credit card. Business deposits sat next to personal deposits; parts charges sat next to groceries. You could sort them by hand, but that is not a clean way to operate going forward. The LLC needs its own accounts.
What You’ll Discover
  • How the LLC gets an identifying number the bank and IRS can recognize.
  • How separate bank and card accounts make the business easier to keep separate going forward.
  • Which step in this sequence actually changes the balance sheet.
