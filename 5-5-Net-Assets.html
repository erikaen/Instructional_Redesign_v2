Module 5: Naming What You Built

Bicycle repair shop · ~15 min

One quick widening before you stop. Your repair work has an owner — you. What about an entity with no owner?

ANNA
“Plenty of real entities have nobody to call an owner — a food bank, a museum, a public university, a church. Real books, real Assets and Liabilities, same identity: Assets − Liabilities = the residual. But the for-profit label Equities grew up where owners exist. With no owner, the convention writes Net Assets instead. Same arithmetic — the residual is still just recorded Assets minus recorded Liabilities. The name changes because the accountability does: your shop answers to you; a food bank answers to its mission and its donors.”
Owned entity
Assets−Liabilities=Equities
Accountable to owners.
No-owner entity
Assets−Liabilities=Net Assets
Accountable to mission & donors.
Same identity, different label

Net Assets is the same kind of definitional residual as Equities — not a pool of cash, and not anyone’s ownership stake. A no-owner entity further splits its Net Assets by whether donors attached strings (with vs. without donor restrictions) — but that machinery, and how to read it, is a later-module story. For now: same identity, different label, because the accountability differs.
