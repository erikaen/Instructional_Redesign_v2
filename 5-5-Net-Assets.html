One quick widening before you stop. Your repair work has an owner — you. What about an entity with no owner?

ANNA
“Plenty of real entities have no owner — a food bank, a museum, a public university, a church. They still keep real books, with real Assets and Liabilities, and the same identity still holds:

Assets − Liabilities = the residual.

For a for-profit entity with owners, we usually call that residual Equities. For an entity with no owner, we usually call it Net Assets instead. The arithmetic is the same: recorded Assets minus recorded Liabilities. The label changes because the entity answers to different people and purposes. Your shop answers to you. A food bank answers to its mission and its donors.”
Owned entity
Assets−Liabilities=Equities
Accountable to owners.
No-owner entity
Assets−Liabilities=Net Assets
Accountable to mission & donors.
Same identity, different label

Net Assets is the same kind of residual as Equities: it is recorded Assets minus recorded Liabilities.

It is not a pool of cash, and it is not anyone’s ownership stake. In a no-owner entity, Net Assets may later be split based on donor restrictions — for example, whether donors gave money for a specific purpose. But that is a later topic.

For now, remember the main idea: same identity, different label, because the entity is accountable in a different way.
