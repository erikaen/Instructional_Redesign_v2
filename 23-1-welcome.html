The Operating Agreement

The Certificate made the LLC exist. Now there’s a second document — the one that says how it works. You ask Anna if she has ever seen one.

ANNA
I helped write City Cycle’s. Every LLC needs an Operating Agreement — the rulebook: who the member is, what they put in, who’s on the hook. But it’s yours, with your facts.”

“Here’s City Cycle’s, just so you can see the shape of it —
OPERATING AGREEMENT
OF CITY CYCLE, LLC
Anna’s example
§7  Sole Member. The sole member is Marcus Webb.
§9  Capital Contributions. The member contributes the shop’s assets and liabilities…
§17  Liability of the Sole Member. The member is not personally liable…
· · ·
ANNA
See the shape? Same sections for every LLC. Yours just carries your name, your business, your numbers. One thing to know going in: the agreement ends with Schedule A — the list of what the member contributes — and you can’t finalize a list of what’s contributed before the contributing is done. So yours starts as a draft. Draft it and we’ll read it together. (You can reopen City Cycle’s example anytime from the button above.)
