The line you drew isn’t the only line
What You Discovered
  • The boundary you have kept in your books is real, but informal. It does not have legal force.
  • Your books treat the repair work as separate from you. The bank, the customer, and the law do not.
  • A liability policy can help pay a claim, but it does not create a legal boundary between the repair work and your personal life.
  • To create that second boundary, you need a different kind of line: a legal one. One common form for that line is an LLC.
The deeper point

You already drew one boundary: an accounting boundary in your books. That boundary lets you keep track of what the repair work has, what it owes, and why its residual changed. But it is still a boundary in the records.

What you do not have yet is a second boundary beside it: a legal one. The two boundaries are drawn for different reasons, and they do different jobs. Next, you will draw the legal one.
