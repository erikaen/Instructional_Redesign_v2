## 53-1-Two-Balance-Sheets.html — Two Balance Sheets
### What this page is
The walk's opening move (~4 min, 4 steps): CVS's Consolidated Balance Sheets as printed in the 10-K (one table, two date columns) are stripped to the three totals the identity uses, split into beginning and ending snapshots with a year between them, and re-expanded — the exact split-and-explain move the student performed on the bike shop's snapshots, now at $250-billion scale. It follows 52-1 (the map refresher) and precedes 53-2 (the cash question).

### The page, step by step
Step 0: the document form — "Here is the Consolidated Balance Sheets table as it appears in CVS's 10-K (page 95). One table, two date columns — 2025 and 2024 — and item-level detail across roughly thirty rows. The accounting identity holds at both dates." The full ~30-row table renders with 2025 and 2024 value columns.

Step 1: strip to totals — the architecture works on three numbers per date (Total assets, Total liabilities, Total shareholders' equity) because that is what the identity uses; the non-total rows are set aside. An identity-check box confirms both dates: 2025: $253,538 − $178,156 = $75,382 ✓; 2024: $253,215 − $177,485 = $75,730 ✓.

Step 2: split into snapshots — the 2024 column becomes the beginning Balance Sheet on the left, the 2025 column the ending Balance Sheet on the right, with a center card: "A year between them. The Income Statement, the Statement of Shareholders' Equity, and the Cash Flow Statement belong in this space." Three explanatory statements will account for what happened in the year.

Step 3: the line-item detail returns in two independent snapshot Balance Sheets, the gap in the middle ready for the three statements. The final control chains to 53-2 ("The Cash Question →").

## 53-2-The-Cash-Question.html — The Cash Question
### What this page is
The cash arc opens (~6 min, 5 steps): the Balance Sheets compact to a few asset lines, cash is highlighted, and a question card asks what happened to it (down $133 million against roughly $400 billion of revenue); then CVS's three-year Cash Flow Statement is met in document form and collapsed, column by column and row by row, to its bottom-line answer — which is $172 million, not $133. The mismatch is left standing for 53-3.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: "A new question. CVS's recorded cash went down by $133 million. Out of nearly $254 billion in recorded assets, where did the cash go?" Both Balance Sheets compact to four asset lines — Cash, Other current assets, Other assets, and All other noncash assets — with Total liabilities and Total shareholders' equity below; the identity still holds; Cash is highlighted on both dates ($8,586 → $8,453). The center question card: "What happened to cash?" — $133 million decrease; setup: CVS earned roughly $400 billion in revenue this year, and cash on the Balance Sheet barely moved — where did money come from, and where did it go? Footer: the Cash Flow Statement is the explanatory statement that traces what happened to cash.

Step 1: the document-form Cash Flow Statement (10-K page 96), Balance Sheets stepped aside. Three years, three full sets of operating, investing, and financing line items. (CVS reports the **direct method** — actual receipts and payments by category.)

Step 2: each CFS column covers one fiscal year; the 2025 column covers January 1 through December 31, 2025 — exactly the span between the two Balance Sheets. The other two columns connect nothing being held; they drop out of the picture.

Step 3: inside the 2025 column the line items collapse to the section subtotals plus the reconciliation footer — Operating $10,639, Investing $(5,871), Financing $(4,940) — three sections that sum to a single change in cash, $(172).

Step 4: even the subtotals fall away: "cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash decreased by $172 million during 2025." The label is longer than the Balance Sheet's, and the number is not $133 — that tension carries into the next page. Chains to 53-3 ("Read the Labels →").

### If the student is lost
- "Why compact the Balance Sheets?" Orient: which single line is the question about? Nudge: everything else is set aside so Cash can be watched at both dates; the two "Other" lines stay visible for a reason that pays off next page. Stronger hint: restricted cash lives inside Other current assets and Other assets — 53-3 needs those rows.
- "Why drop two of the three columns?" Orient: which year sits between the two snapshots being held? Nudge: only the 2025 column covers the span between December 31, 2024 and December 31, 2025. Stronger hint: the other columns cover earlier spans that connect neither snapshot — same move the walk makes on every statement.
- "What are Operating / Investing / Financing?" Orient: the student met these in Module 4 — which bucket held the bike shop's card payoff? Nudge: classification follows the liability settled; the three sections sum to the change in cash. Stronger hint: use the page's glossary — the architecture is three sections summing to one bottom line, here +10,639 − 5,871 − 4,940 = −172.
- Tutor conduct: do NOT resolve the 133-vs-172 tension — 53-3 stages it. If a student notices early, praise the notice and point them forward, not to the answer.

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY.
- Card: cash $8,586 → $8,453, down $133M (BS scope). CFS 2025: Operating 10,639 / Investing (5,871) / Financing (4,940) → net change (172); bottom line reads "cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash decreased by $172 million."
- The mismatch (133 vs 172) is deliberately unresolved on this page.
- CVS uses the direct method; about 98% of US public companies use the indirect method — the page's glossary carries this.
- Glossary: Cash Flow Statement; Operating activities; Direct method; Restricted cash.

## 53-3-Read-the-Labels.html — Read the Labels
### What this page is
The scope lesson (~5 min, 4 steps): the Balance Sheet says cash fell $133; the Cash Flow Statement says $172; two strips make the mismatch unmissable, and the resolution is that the two lines carry different labels — "Cash and cash equivalents" versus "Cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash." Restricted cash sits inside Other current assets and Other assets; add it back at both dates and the strips reconcile exactly. The architecture is then restored with the CFS in the middle (its three sections appearing in a timed sequence), and the sections become click-to-expand. Every explanatory statement accounts for a change defined by its own scope — read the labels.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: two strips, one mismatch — the Balance Sheet's cash strip (beginning, change −$133, ending) above the Cash Flow Statement's strip (−$172, different beginning and ending balances too). "The labels are different — read them carefully — but the numbers don't match. Something is off." A notice card flags it; the student is invited to look closely before moving on.

Step 1: the solution. The Balance Sheet line reports "Cash and cash equivalents"; the Cash Flow Statement reports "Cash, cash equivalents and restricted cash" — a broader category. The difference is restricted cash sitting inside Other current assets and Other assets on the Balance Sheet face. Add it back at both dates and the strips reconcile exactly ($8,586 + 95 + 203 = $8,884 beginning; $8,453 + 59 + 200 = $8,712 ending; down $172). Each explanatory statement accounts for a specific change defined by its own scope.

Step 2: strips give way to the architecture — beginning Balance Sheet left, ending right, the 2025 CFS between them, the restricted-cash-bearing rows still highlighted. The three sections appear one at a time (a timed reveal): Operating, Investing, Financing, summing to the change in cash.

Step 3: each CFS section is now expandable — clicking shows the line items behind its subtotal, actual cash receipts and payments by category (direct method; roughly 98% of US public companies use the indirect method — CVS is unusual). Chains to 53-4 ("The Equity Question →").

### If the student is lost
- "Which number is wrong, 133 or 172?" Orient: read the two labels aloud — are they the same words? Nudge: neither number is wrong; they answer differently-scoped questions. Stronger hint: the CFS's label adds restricted cash; add it back at both dates and the two strips agree exactly.
- "Where is restricted cash on the Balance Sheet?" Orient: which two compact rows stayed visible since 53-2? Nudge: it sits inside them, not in the Cash line. Stronger hint: Other current assets ($95 → $59 of restricted cash) and Other assets ($203 → $200).
- "The sections appeared one at a time — did I miss something?" Orient: that is the page's own reveal pacing; going Back and Next replays the step with everything shown. Nudge: the three subtotals sum to the net change. Stronger hint: +10,639 − 5,871 − 4,940 = −172.
- Tutor conduct: this page's lesson IS the never-blurt beat of the arc — let the student sit with "something is off" at step 0; confirm the resolution only after they reach step 1. Encourage clicking sections open at step 3 rather than describing their contents.

## 53-4-The-Equity-Question.html — The Equity Question
### What this page is
The equity arc (~8 min, 8 steps): a question card notices that CVS reported roughly $1.7 billion of net income yet total shareholders' equity went *down* $348 million; the single equity row on each Balance Sheet is bucketed into Contributed, Earned & Retained, and Noncontrolling Interests; and the Statement of Shareholders' Equity — met in document form, narrowed to 2025, bucketed, and traced movement by movement with running totals — accounts for every dollar. The corporate bridge statement is the analogue of the bike shop's Statement of Changes in Member's Capital.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: the question card — "What happened to equity?" $75,730 → $75,382, down $348 million; setup: "CVS reported Net income of about $1.7 billion this year — yet total shareholders' equity went down. What moved it?" Footer: the Statement of Shareholders' Equity traces every change in equity, from any source. The compact Balance Sheets flank the card with the equity rows highlighted.

Step 1: bucket the equity — the single Total shareholders' equity row on each Balance Sheet expands into three buckets: **Contributed** (Common Stock & Capital Surplus net of Treasury), **Earned & Retained** (Retained Earnings plus AOCI), and **Noncontrolling Interests** (the minority equity in consolidated subsidiaries). Between beginning and end: Contributed rose $769, Earned & Retained fell $1,115, NCI fell $2 — summing to the −$348 on the card.

Step 2: the document-form Statement of Shareholders' Equity (10-K page 98) — three years of equity activity, nine columns (share counts, five equity components, subtotals, NCI, total). Every change in equity, from any source — earnings, dividends, stock-based compensation, share repurchases, OCI, NCI activity — flows through this statement.

Step 3: each year is a horizontal band bracketed by a beginning and ending balance row; the 2023 and 2024 bands drop away, leaving the December 31, 2024 balance, the 2025 activity, and the December 31, 2025 balance.

Step 4: the component columns collapse into the same three buckets as the Balance Sheet — Contributed, Earned & Retained, NCI, plus a Total column.

Step 5: the trace — four aggregated 2025 movement rows fire one at a time with running totals updating under each bucket: comprehensive income (Net income plus OCI) lands in Earned & Retained with a −$40 slice in NCI (the loss attributable to subsidiary minority owners — flagged here, formally explained on the Income Statement page); stock option activity, awards, and ESPP land in Contributed; dividends pull Earned & Retained down. The bottom lines equal the Balance Sheet deltas (+769 / −1,115 / −2).

Step 6: architecture restored — the 2025 statement slots between the two Balance Sheets, each bucketed equity row connecting to its bucket column.

Step 7: the buckets become expandable — clicking a bucket header shows the original 10-K columns underneath (Contributed → Common Stock & Capital Surplus and Treasury Stock; Earned & Retained → Retained Earnings and AOCI). Chains to 53-5 ("Where Net Income Comes From →").

### If the student is lost
- "How can equity fall in a profitable year?" Orient: does net income move equity alone? Nudge: the statement traces every change, from any source — earnings are one row among several. Stronger hint: dividends pulled Earned & Retained down by more than comprehensive income added; the buckets show +769 / −1,115 / −2 netting to −348.
- "What are the three buckets?" Orient: what were the bike shop's piles? Nudge: same classification idea at corporate scale — what owners put in, what operations generated and kept, plus the interest belonging to others. Stronger hint: Contributed = Common Stock & Capital Surplus net of Treasury; Earned & Retained = Retained Earnings + AOCI; NCI = minority equity in consolidated subsidiaries.
- "What's the −$40 in NCI?" Orient: the page flags it and says where it formally lives. Nudge: it is the loss attributable to subsidiary minority owners. Stronger hint: hold it — the Income Statement page (53-5) splits net income between CVS shareholders and NCI; confirm it there, not here.
- "What is AOCI?" Orient: which bucket does it sit in? Nudge: it collects OCI — gains and losses that bypass net income but still change equity; the next page shows the $526 that landed there. Stronger hint: use the glossary; full treatment arrives on 53-5.
- Tutor conduct: this is the bike shop's bridge-statement lesson at scale — analogize to Contributed/Generated/Withdrawn freely (that is the page's own framing). Let the running-total trace fire; don't recite the landing numbers before the rows have fired.

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY.
- Card: 75,730 → 75,382, −348. Buckets: Contributed +769; Earned & Retained −1,115; NCI −2; sum −348.
- Trace rows land at the Balance Sheet deltas; comprehensive income = NI + OCI into E&R, −40 into NCI; options/awards/ESPP into Contributed; dividends reduce E&R.

## 53-5-Where-Net-Income-Comes-From.html — Where Net Income Comes From
### What this page is
The income arc (~8 min, 8 steps): on the Statement of Shareholders' Equity, Net income was a single movement row; this page opens the Income Statement behind it — Revenue of $402 billion down to Net income $1,728, split between CVS shareholders ($1,768) and noncontrolling interests (−$40) — then adds the Statement of Comprehensive Income's $526 of OCI and lands each piece in its equity bucket: $1,768 + $526 = $2,294 into Earned & Retained, −$40 into NCI. This is where the module's "two net incomes" promise is kept.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: the highlight moves to the Earned & Retained rows on the bucketed Balance Sheets; the question card: "Where did Net Income come from?" — "On the Statement of Shareholders' Equity, Net income was a single movement row. Behind that one number sits a $402-billion year of operations." Footer: the Income Statement traces how revenues and costs produced Net income.

Step 1: the document-form Consolidated Statements of Operations (10-K page 93) — Revenue at the top ($402 billion in 2025), operating costs, non-operating items, then Net income at the bottom, split between CVS shareholders and noncontrolling interests; three years.

Step 2: the same move as the CFS and SoSE — the 2025 column covers the span between the two Balance Sheets; the other columns drop out.

Step 3: the line items collapse to the skeleton: Total revenues → Total operating costs → Operating income → non-operating items → Income before tax → Income tax → Net income → the NCI line → Net income attributable to CVS Health.

Step 4: three numbers remain: Net income $1,728 (consolidated), Net (income) loss attributable to NCI $40 (the loss the subsidiaries' minority owners absorbed), and Net income attributable to CVS Health $1,768. The $40 flagged on the SoSE formally lives here.

Step 5: Net income is not the whole story of equity-affecting earnings — the separate Statement of Comprehensive Income (10-K page 94) adds five OCI items (unrealized investment gains, foreign currency, hedges, pensions) summing to $526; Net income plus OCI equals Comprehensive income of $2,254. The $526 is what landed in AOCI.

Step 6: the Income Statement narrows to a strip showing where each piece lands: $1,768 into Earned & Retained (Retained Earnings), $526 into Earned & Retained (AOCI) — together $2,294, exactly the Comprehensive income attributable to CVS Health — and −$40 into NCI.

Step 7: architecture restored — the 2025 Income Statement slots between the Balance Sheets (Revenue $402B down to NI attributable $1,768, plus the OCI piece $526); the CFS and SoSE still belong in the middle column. Chains to 53-6 ("The Full Architecture →").

### If the student is lost
- "Why are there two net incomes?" Orient: who owns the subsidiaries CVS consolidates? Nudge: not all of them are wholly owned — the statements must show the interest belonging to others. Stronger hint: consolidated Net income $1,728 plus the $40 loss absorbed by minority owners equals the $1,768 attributable to CVS Health — and 51-1's preview promised exactly this complication.
- "What is OCI / comprehensive income?" Orient: did every equity-affecting gain pass through Net income? Nudge: five items bypass it but still change equity; they sum to $526. Stronger hint: NI $1,728 + OCI $526 = Comprehensive income $2,254; the attributable-to-CVS slice is $1,768 + $526 = $2,294, which is what the SoSE's comprehensive-income row carried into Earned & Retained. (M5-LO5 wants awareness here, not mastery.)
- "Where does the $40 come from?" Orient: it was flagged on 53-4 — which statement formally splits it? Nudge: the Income Statement's bottom rows. Stronger hint: Net (income) loss attributable to NCI $40 sits between Net income $1,728 and NI attributable to CVS Health $1,768.
- Tutor conduct: keep the bike-shop contrast available — the shop had one net income and no OCI; noticing the absence was the point (51-1, point 5). Confirm figures only as the collapse steps reveal them.

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY.
- Chain: 1,728 (consolidated NI) + 40 (NCI loss) = 1,768 (attributable to CVS); OCI = 526; 1,728 + 526 = 2,254 comprehensive; 1,768 + 526 = 2,294 comprehensive attributable to CVS, = the E&R landing; −40 lands in NCI.
- Steps: 0 card (E&R rows highlighted) · 1 doc-form p.93 · 2 drop columns · 3 skeleton · 4 bottom three numbers · 5 OCI (p.94) · 6 strip → buckets · 7 architecture restored.
- Glossary: Income Statement; Operating income; Comprehensive income; Noncontrolling interests.

### Anchor
anchor: On the Statement of Shareholders’ Equity, Net income was a single movement row. Behind that one number sits a $402-billion year of operations.
