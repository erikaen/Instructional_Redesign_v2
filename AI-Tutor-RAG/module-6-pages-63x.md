## 63-1-The-Statement-of-Financial-Position.html — The Statement of Financial Position
### What this page is
The walk's opening (~6 min, 5 steps): Yale's Consolidated Statements of Financial Position in document form; a full-stop beat on what is NOT on the statement (the nonprofit recognition lesson); the strip to the three totals with the identity checked at both dates; and the split into beginning and ending snapshots with a fiscal year between them. The Module-6 twin of 53-1, with the What's-NOT beat as its extra step. Follows 62-1; precedes 63-2.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: document form — "Here is Yale's Consolidated Statements of Financial Position as it appears on page 21 of the FY2025 financial report. One table, two date columns — June 30, 2025 on the left, June 30, 2024 on the right — exactly as Yale presents it." The architecture is the known one: a snapshot of recorded assets, recorded liabilities, and the difference between them; Yale's name for that difference is **Net Assets**, because a nonprofit has no owners; the identity is still A − L = Net Assets. (Figures rounded to $ millions; the report states thousands.) The table shows all rows, including the Net Assets block with its noncontrolling-interests sub-line.

Step 1: **"What's NOT on this Statement" — "Recorded ≠ everything Yale holds."** An amber card replaces the table with four items: **Collections** — the Beinecke's rare books and manuscripts, the Art Gallery, the Center for British Art, the Peabody's specimens — billions in fair value and zero on the books, excluded under the FASB **ASC 958-360** election (collections held for public exhibition, education, or research; protected and preserved; sale proceeds reinvested in the collections); Yale makes that election and the footnotes disclose it; the statement itself is silent. **Human capital** — the faculty, students, and staff, excluded for every organization, nonprofit and for-profit alike. **The Yale name** — decisive for admissions, donations, and hiring; off the books under the rule that excludes internally developed intangibles from any balance sheet — the same reason CVS's brand is off CVS's books. **Old buildings at cost** — the recorded $6,340M is cost less accumulated depreciation; for stone buildings acquired or built generations ago that figure does not describe what the campus would command today. Closing line: each is a **feature of the recognition convention** — a deliberate choice with identifiable trade-offs, not a failure of ability. The identity still works on whatever the convention captures.

Step 2: strip to totals — Total assets, Total liabilities, Total net assets — with the identity box: 2025: $59,043 − $9,672 = $49,371 ✓; 2024: $55,315 − $9,278 = $46,037 ✓; "A − L = Net Assets · the identity holds at both dates."

Step 3: split — the June 30, 2024 column becomes the Beginning SFP on the left; June 30, 2025 the Ending SFP on the right; the center card: "A fiscal year between them. The Cash Flow Statement and the Statement of Activities belong in this space" — **two** statements will account for the year.

Step 4: the detail returns in two independent snapshots. Tail insight: two snapshots of the identity, one fiscal year apart, and a gap two statements will explain. Chains to 63-2 ("The Cash Question →").

## 63-3-Read-the-Labels.html — Read the Labels
### What this page is
The scope lesson, Yale edition (~5 min, 4 steps): the SFP says cash fell $293M; the CFS says $380M; the resolution is Note c — the CFS reconciles a broader figure that adds *cash included in investments, at fair value* (cash parked in Yale's long-term investment pool awaiting deployment). The architecture is then restored with the CFS between the SFPs (timed section reveal), and the sections become expandable — Operating showing the full indirect-method walk. The same read-the-labels lesson as CVS's restricted cash, with a different mechanism behind the label.

### The page, step by step
Step 0: two strips, one mismatch — the SFP cash strip (−$293: $1,058 → $765) above the CFS strip (−$380: $1,205 → $825). "Different numbers, different beginning and ending balances. Look closely at the two labels before moving on — the difference is not an error." A red notice card restates both.

Step 1: the solution — Note c, page 27 of the report. The SFP reports *Cash and cash equivalents* ($765 / $1,058); the CFS adds *cash included in investments, at fair value* — cash sitting inside Yale's long-term investment pool awaiting deployment ($61 at year-end, $147 at the start) — to reach $825 / $1,205. A green solution card shows the two-column reconciliation (1,058 + 147 = 1,205; 765 + 61 = 825, with the footnote that in the report's thousands the tie is exact at both dates: 1,058,065 + 146,776 = 1,204,841; 764,569 + 60,558 = 825,127 — rounding to millions leaves the $1M remainder visible at the year-end date).
