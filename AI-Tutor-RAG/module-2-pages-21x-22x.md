# Module 2, pages 21x–22x — RAG knowledge base for the AI Tutor

This file covers the two Module-2 tutorials that open the “Getting Formal” arc:
Tutorial 2.1 (The Two You’s, pages 21-1 through 21-5) and Tutorial 2.2
(Filing for a Legal Body, pages 22-1 through 22-5). Sections appear in the
order a student encounters them.

Tutor conduct encoded throughout: the course teaches by DISCOVERY, so the tutor
orients with questions and gives progressively stronger hints, mirroring each
page’s own three-strike spirit (hint, stronger hint, reveal). The tutor never
announces what a page is about to reveal. In particular the tutor never states,
before a page does, that personal money quietly covered repair-work costs in
Module 1; never gives a name to the Assets-minus-Liabilities difference from
Module 1 (it is only “the difference” there); never uses the name “Member’s
Capital” before Module 2 earns it; never previews Module 3’s depreciation
discovery; and never says that revenue and expenses are a sorting of the
Reasons tab’s generated rows. The tutor says “accounting identity,” never
“accounting equation”; never the words “equity”/“equities” or “Net Assets”;
treats the repair work as an activity (it cannot own, owe, or agree); treats
documents and time periods as non-actors; and says records SHOW things while
entities HAVE things.

Interface facts the tutor can rely on: every page has a header with a progress
bar, a glossary button, and a restart button; Enter submits typed answers;
Continue buttons gate advancement and unlock when the page’s task completes;
sorting tasks put items on the left and category zones on the right, and a
“Do the rest”/“Sort the rest” control appears after a few correct placements;
a hidden triple-click dev mode exists but is INSTRUCTOR-ONLY — never point a
student to it.

## 21-1-welcome.html — The Call from Maria

### What this page is
This is the welcome (Phase 1 of 5) for Tutorial 2.1, “The Two You’s.” It opens
the module’s “Getting Formal” arc by reframing the repair work: it has grown,
and Maria the insurance agent now calls back with a concern that is NOT about
property. The page sets up three discoveries the student will make across the
tutorial and then hands off to the exposure phase. Nothing interactive is
required here beyond reading and pressing Begin.

### The page, step by step
1. Narrative block reminds the student that the books (Bike-Repair.xlsx) have
   kept the bike-repair role separate from the rest of life, and introduces
   Maria’s new worry: a customer getting hurt and holding the student
   personally responsible.
2. A “What you’ll discover” card lists three bullets: what Maria is really
   warning about and why insurance alone won’t solve it; who besides the student
   treats the bike-repair role as separate; and what it would take to make that
   separation more than hand-maintained.
3. A single **Begin →** button at the bottom navigates to 21-2-exposure.html.
   There is no task to complete; the button is immediately available.

### If the student is lost
- “I don’t get what changed from Module 1.”
  - Orient: “What did your books already do for you in Module 1?”
  - Nudge: “Maria is calling back — is she worried about the same thing as
    last time, or something different?”
  - Stronger hint: “Last time it was your tools and parts. This time she says
    the word ‘liability.’ What is she NOT talking about?”
- “Why is insurance not enough?”
  - Orient: “What does an insurance policy do — does it build something, or
    does it pay something?”
  - Nudge: “Read the three bullets. One of them says insurance alone won’t
    solve it. What does it point to instead?”
  - Stronger hint: “A policy pays a claim. It does not build a wall. The page
    is setting up a second, different kind of line. Don’t name it yet — keep
    reading.”

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY: This page has no graded answer; it is a framing page. The key idea
the student should carry forward is that Maria’s concern is LEGAL LIABILITY
(a claim reaching personal assets), not property damage, and that the tutorial
will argue insurance pays claims but does not create legal protection. The
glossary already names the eventual answer — an LLC (Limited Liability
Company) — but the tutor should not volunteer it; the page’s own bullets only
tease “what it would take to make that separation more than something you
maintain by hand.” Confirm only after the student has read and clicked Begin.
There is no three-strike reveal on this page.

### Anchor
anchor: What Maria is really warning you about, and why an insurance policy alone won’t solve it.

### Before and after
Before: the student finished Module 1, where the books drew an accounting
boundary around the repair work. After: the student goes to 21-2 to see, concretely, where a liability claim can reach when no legal wall exists.

## 21-2-exposure.html — Follow the Claim

### What this page is
Phase 2 of 5. Maria explains that property coverage protects tools and parts
but does not solve LIABILITY. The student opens two lists — things recorded for
the repair work and personal assets — and then sends Smith’s claim to watch it
cross straight through the (non-existent) legal wall into personal assets.
This page produces the felt experience that “the boundary in your books is not
a wall,” which motivates the rest of the tutorial.

### The page, step by step
1. Maria’s dialog box states that property coverage does not solve liability,
   and asks what a claim from Smith can reach.
2. A figure appears with two boxes side by side, separated by a dashed gap
   labeled implicitly as “no legal wall yet.” Each box has a reveal button.
3. The student clicks **Assets recorded for the repair work ↓** to reveal:
   Repair equipment, Computer equipment, Supplies, Fixtures.
4. The student clicks **Personal assets not recorded in the books ↓** to
   reveal: Your car, Your earnings, Your savings.
5. The student clicks the red **Send Smith’s claim →** button. A “thud” sound
   plays; both boxes turn red; a message reads “the claim reaches the repair
   work — and crosses straight through to you. The boundary in your books is
   not a wall.” The personal box shows “× exposed — nothing stops the claim.”
6. Maria returns with a second dialog: books separate the repair work from
   personal life but do not create legal protection; liability coverage can
   pay a claim but only PAYS, it does not proactively BUILD protection; that is
   where an LLC comes in.
7. A **Continue →** button unlocks and goes to 21-3-whoSeesTwo.html.

### If the student is lost
- “I opened the lists — now what?”
  - Orient: “There’s one more button in red. What does it tell you to do?”
  - Nudge: “You’ve seen where each list sits. The button sends the claim.
    What do you think happens to the gap between the boxes?”
  - Stronger hint: “Press ‘Send Smith’s claim’ and watch the dashed line and
    the personal box. The page is about to show you why books aren’t a wall.”
- “Why didn’t the claim stop at the repair work?”
  - Orient: “What is between the two boxes — a solid wall or a dashed line?”
  - Nudge: “Your books separated things, but did anything legal stand in the
    gap?”
  - Stronger hint: “The dashed line means no legal wall. The claim walks
    through it. Maria says it in her second message — read what she says about
    what a policy does versus what an LLC does.”

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY: The repair-work list is Repair equipment, Computer equipment,
Supplies, Fixtures. The personal list is Your car, Your earnings, Your savings.
After sending the claim, the correct reading is: the claim reaches the repair
work AND crosses through to personal assets because the books’ boundary is not
a legal wall. Maria’s third-strike-style reveal (her second dialog) states that
liability coverage can help pay a claim but only PAYS it; it does not
proactively build protection — an LLC is what builds the wall. The tutor hints
first and only confirms this after the student has sent the claim and read
Maria’s message. Do not blurt “LLC” before Maria does.

### Anchor
anchor: Open each list to see where Smith’s claim can reach.

### Before and after
Before: the student read the welcome framing. After: having felt the claim
pierce the books-only boundary, the student goes to 21-3 to ask who actually
sees the repair work as separate.

## 21-3-whoSeesTwo.html — Who Sees Two?

### What this page is
Phase 3 of 5. The student sorts five “observers” into two zones: those that
see the bike-repair role as separate from the rest of life, and those that see
one undivided you. Only the books see two; the bank, the credit-card company,
Smith, and the law each see one. The completed sort reveals that the boundary
is real in the records but informal and legally unenforceable — which is why
Smith’s claim did not stop.

### The page, step by step
1. A narrative notes the books already hold a boundary, then asks why Smith’s
   claim did not stop at the repair work.
2. A callout instructs: drag each card into the zone where it belongs — does
   this view treat the bike-repair role as separate?
3. A drag bank on the left holds five cards: Your books (Bike-Repair.xlsx),
   The bank (Your checking account), The credit-card company (Harborline Visa),
   Smith (Your customer), The law (A court).
4. Two drop zones on the right:
   - “Sees your bike-repair role as separate from the rest of your life.”
   - “Sees one undivided you.”
5. The student drags each card. Correct placements play a success chime and
   lock in with a reason sentence; wrong placements play a “nope” tone and the
   card returns to the bank.
6. When all five are placed correctly, an insight box appears: “Only the Books
   See the Boundary,” explaining the separation is informal and self-maintained
   with no legal force. A **Continue →** button goes to 21-4-twoHats.html.

### If the student is lost
- “I keep dropping a card and it bounces back.”
  - Orient: “A bounce means the zone is wrong. What is each zone asking you to
    decide?”
  - Nudge: “For the card that bounced, ask: does this observer treat your
    repair work as separate, or do they just see you?”
  - Stronger hint: “The bank account and the credit card are in YOUR personal
    name. The receipt carries YOUR name. The law sees one person. Which zone
    is ‘one undivided you’?”
- “Is Smith separate — I did the repair for them.”
  - Orient: “Whose name is on the receipt Smith got?”
  - Nudge: “From Smith’s view, is there a second ‘repair work’ person to deal
    with, or just you?”
  - Stronger hint: “Smith hired you personally. There is no separate
    bike-repair entity from Smith’s view. That’s the ‘sees one’ zone.”
- “Why is only the books ‘separate’?”
  - Orient: “Who drew the boundary in the books?”
  - Nudge: “Did any of the other four observers agree to it, or did you keep
    it yourself?”
  - Stronger hint: “You sorted co-mingled transactions by purpose — YOU did
    that, in your own records. No legal document says the repair work stands
    apart yet. That’s what the insight box says after you finish.”

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY: Correct placements —
- Your books → “sees your bike-repair role as separate” (sees TWO).
- The bank → “sees one undivided you” (account is in your personal name).
- The credit-card company → “sees one undivided you” (card in your personal
  name; no second borrower).
- Smith → “sees one undivided you” (receipt carries your name).
- The law → “sees one undivided you” (one person; no legal document separating
  the repair work).
The completion insight (the page’s reveal) states: only the books treat the
role as separate; the recognition is informal, self-maintained, and has no
legal force — which is why Smith’s claim did not stop. Confirm only after
genuine attempts; mirror the page by letting wrong drops return to the bank
rather than feeding the answer.

### Anchor
anchor: Drag each card into the zone where it belongs.

### Before and after
Before: the student watched a claim pierce the books-only boundary. After:
having sorted who sees two versus one, the student goes to 21-4 to learn what
the second line — a legal one — would be.

## 21-4-twoHats.html — Same Head, Two Hats

### What this page is
Phase 4 of 5. A short narrative names what has been true all along: there are
two roles — you in the rest of life, and you in the bike-repair role. The
student then presses a single button to reveal “the second line”: the
accounting boundary in the books is real but is where YOU recognized the role;
what comes next is FORMALIZING it with a legal line beside the accounting one,
giving the role a body of its own. The page names the LLC as one common way to
draw that legal line and points to forming one next.

### The page, step by step
1. A narrative states there have been two roles all along, and that the
   recognition still lives inside the records — the bank, the credit-card
   company, Smith, and the law do not yet treat the role as separate.
2. One button appears: **Why wasn’t the boundary enough?**
3. The student clicks it. A success chime plays and an insight box titled
   “The second line” appears. It explains: the books’ boundary is an
   ACCOUNTING boundary — where you recognized the role; what comes next is
   formalizing it with a legal line so the bank, card company, Smith, and the
   law must recognize it. The order is always: recognize first, then
   formalize. Maria already named one common way — an LLC — and next the
   student will form one.
4. A **Continue →** button unlocks and goes to 21-5-complete.html.

### If the student is lost
- “What am I supposed to do here?”
  - Orient: “How many buttons are on the page?”
  - Nudge: “There’s one button asking a question. Press it and read what
    appears.”
  - Stronger hint: “Click ‘Why wasn’t the boundary enough?’ The page will tell
    you what kind of line the books drew, and what kind still needs drawing.”
- “What’s the ‘second line’?”
  - Orient: “What kind of boundary do your books already give you?”
  - Nudge: “The insight box contrasts two lines. One you drew in the books.
    What does it say the other line is, and who must recognize it?”
  - Stronger hint: “The first line is accounting — in your records. The second
    is legal — so that the bank, the card company, Smith, and the law must
    recognize the role. The box names an LLC as one way to draw it. Let the
    page say it; don’t get ahead of it.”

### TUTOR-ONLY answer key
TUTOR-ONLY: The single action is pressing “Why wasn’t the boundary enough?”
The reveal (“The second line” insight box) states: the books’ boundary is an
accounting boundary where YOU recognized the role; formalizing means a legal
line beside it that the bank, credit-card company, Smith, and the law must
recognize; the order is always recognize-then-formalize; Maria already named an
LLC as one common way; next the student forms one. The tutor should not state
“LLC” or “legal entity” before the page does — orient with questions about the
two lines, then confirm the page’s wording after the student has opened the
box. There is no three-strike pattern beyond this single reveal.

### Anchor
anchor: Why wasn’t the boundary enough?

### Before and after
Before: the student discovered only the books see the boundary, and it is
informal. After: the student goes to 21-5 to review the tutorial’s
discoveries before forming an LLC in Tutorial 2.2.
