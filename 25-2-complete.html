What You Discovered
  • You saw that the repair work was already something the books could describe separately from your personal life.
  • You formed an LLC, giving the repair work a legal name and legal structure.
  • You created an Operating Agreement, signed in two roles, and showed the existing business as contributed to the Company.
  • You gave the LLC its own EIN, bank account, and card, so the separation is easier to maintain going forward.
  • You laid out the LLC’s balance sheet: total assets of $3,910, total liabilities of $1,450, and total equities of $2,460.
The One Idea to Keep

The LLC did not create the repair work from nothing. Before the LLC, your books could already describe the repair work as an accounting entity: what it had, what it owed, and the difference between the two.

What Module 2 added was formal structure around that business: a legal name, rules, an EIN, and accounts in the LLC’s name. That setup matters because it gives the business a clean starting point going forward. From here, the LLC starts with assets of $3,910, liabilities of $1,450, and equities of $2,460.
